Priwall ferry

Where does the Priwall ferry run?

  1. 01 Car ferry: Vorderreihe ↔ Priwall The short crossing in the town centre – for cars, bikes and passengers, about five minutes. Across lie the Passat and the Priwall beach.
  2. 02 Passenger ferry: Nordermole ↔ Priwall The second, more northerly line at the Nordermole – passengers and bikes only, no cars. It runs year-round with seasonal hours (until 10:30 p.m. in summer, 8 p.m. in winter); the return from the Priwall leaves immediately after arrival.

Map geometry: OpenStreetMap (ODbL).

Who travels for free?

Priwall residents with their main residence on the peninsula cross free of charge, as does hand luggage (bags, cases, folding bikes). Everyone else pays the tariff. Regular commuters travel far more cheaply with a weekly, monthly or annual pass than with single tickets. Arriving by car? Find parking and petrol stations in town beforehand.

Other ways to the Priwall

  • Priwall ferry (Stadtwerke Lübeck) The main car and passenger ferry from the Vorderreihe – year-round, at short intervals.
  • Norderfähre A separate passenger ferry for pedestrians and cyclists a little further north at the Nordermole – year-round, with seasonal operating hours (shorter in winter).
  • By car without the ferry Only via the long land route through Mecklenburg (eastern side, towards Boltenhagen/Rostock, via the A20) – impractical for a short visit.

Frequently asked questions about the Priwall ferry

What does the Priwall ferry cost in 2026?

A single crossing costs 2,10 € per person and 5,10 € for a car up to 3.5 t, a bicycle 1,20 € (as of 1 January 2026, Stadtwerke Lübeck). Frequent travellers save with multi-trip, weekly, monthly or annual passes.

Who travels on the Priwall ferry for free?

Priwall residents with their main residence on the peninsula travel free, as does hand luggage (bags, cases, folding bikes). Everyone else pays the tariff.

When does the Priwall ferry run?

The car ferry runs around the clock, 365 days a year: during the day (about 5 a.m. to 10:45 p.m.) it shuttles every 10 to 15 minutes; at night it departs Travemünde on a fixed schedule – every quarter-hour from 11 p.m. to midnight and 3 to 4 a.m., every half-hour from midnight to 2 a.m.; from the Priwall it returns immediately after arrival. Special timetables – e.g. during Travemünde Week – are on the operator’s timetable page (as of 18 July 2026).

Does the Priwall ferry run at night?

Yes. The car ferry also runs through the night – every half-hour between midnight and 2 a.m. (at minute 00 and 30 from Travemünde), every quarter-hour between 11 p.m. and midnight and between 3 and 4 a.m. The separate Nordermole passenger ferry, by contrast, keeps fixed seasonal hours and does not run at night (except for extended times during Travemünde Week and on New Year’s Eve).

How long is the crossing to the Priwall?

Only a few minutes – the Trave is narrow here. From the Vorderreihe landing to the Priwall takes barely longer than boarding and disembarking.

Can you reach the Priwall by car without the ferry?

Only via the long land route through Mecklenburg-Vorpommern (eastern side, via the A20). For visitors from Travemünde the ferry is the only practical way. There is no tunnel to the Priwall.

Can you buy the ferry ticket online or by app?

You pay on board; season tickets and passes are available from Stadtwerke Lübeck. Whether a single ticket can be bought online or via an app is best checked directly with Stadtwerke Lübeck (swhl.de).
