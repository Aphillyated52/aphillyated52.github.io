Which location fits?

01

Near the beach

Short walks to the Kurstrand and promenade; the coastal train stops right by the beach. The classic choice for a beach holiday – book early for July.

→ Train to “Lübeck-Travemünde Strand”, then walk.

Plan a day from here →
02

Town centre and Vorderreihe

In the middle of it: restaurants, the ferry pier and evening life on the Trave at your door. Most flexible without a car.

→ Train to “Hafenbahnhof”, five minutes on foot.

Plan a day from here →
03

Priwall

The quiet side: beach, nature and the Waterfront buildings – separated from the bustle by the Trave, connected by ferry in minutes.

→ Take the car ferry, or cross on foot.

Plan a day from here →
04

Surroundings as base

Lübeck or the bay towns as your base, Travemünde as the day trip – easy by coastal train and often cheaper.

→ Coastal train from Lübeck Hbf, about 20 minutes to the beach.

Where to search?

First stop is the town’s official host directory – owners are listed directly, no middle layer. The big portals additionally carry many private holiday flats; the links here are plain and unpaid.

  • Official host directory (Lübeck-Travemünde Marketing) official, direct travemuende-tourismus.de ↗
  • FeWo-direkt / Vrbo holiday flat portal fewo-direkt.de ↗
  • Booking.com hotels and flats booking.com ↗
  • Airbnb private stays airbnb.com ↗

Plain, unpaid links – this site earns nothing from them. For Travemünde Week (17–26 July), book months ahead.

And how do I get there?

By train to Lübeck-Travemünde Strand or Hafenbahnhof (live departures on the getting-here page), by car via the A1 and B75 – car parks are listed there with a map. For the Priwall: the ferry also takes cars; on foot you are across in minutes.
