Which location fits?

01

Near the beach

Short walks to the Kurstrand and promenade; the coastal train stops right by the beach. The classic choice for a beach holiday – book early for July.

→ Train to “Lübeck-Travemünde Strand”, then walk.

Town centre and Vorderreihe

In the middle of it: restaurants, the ferry pier and evening life on the Trave at your door. Most flexible without a car.

Priwall

Where to search?

First stop is the town’s official host directory – owners are listed directly, no middle layer. The big portals additionally carry many private holiday flats; the links here are plain and unpaid.

If a tent or motorhome is more your style, the Priwall and Ivendorf offer campsites and pitches – gathered on the camping page . For step-free paths, beach access and toilets see the accessibility page.

And how do I get there?

By train to Lübeck-Travemünde Strand or Hafenbahnhof (live departures on the getting-here page), by car via the A1 and B75 – car parks are listed there with a map. For the Priwall: the ferry also takes cars; on foot you are across in minutes.

Frequently asked questions

Where is the best place to stay in Travemünde?

It depends on your trip: near the beach for a classic beach holiday, the town centre and Vorderreihe for restaurants and short car-free walks, the Priwall for quiet and nature. The four locations are described individually above.

Holiday flat or hotel in Travemünde?

Travemünde has plenty of both. Holiday flats are mostly found via the official host directory and the portals; larger hotels are usually near the beach. This page brokers nothing and earns nothing.

When should I book for Travemünde Week?

As early as possible. The festival week (17–26 July 2026) is the most in-demand time of the year – accommodation is often booked out months ahead.

Can you stay on the Priwall?

Yes. With its beach, nature and the Waterfront buildings, the Priwall is the quiet side; the ferry – which also takes cars – gets you into town in minutes.
