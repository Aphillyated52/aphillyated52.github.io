Travemünde from A to Z

A

  • Getting here By train to “Travemünde Strand” right on the promenade, by bus or car (A1/B75) – plus live departures, A1 traffic and the Scandinavia ferries.
  • Doctors and pharmacies Practices, dentists and pharmacies in and around Travemünde with address and verification date – emergency numbers (112, 116117) and the daily pharmacy rota on top.
  • Day trips Lübeck’s UNESCO old town by train, the neighbouring resorts by bus line 40, the Priwall by ferry and the Brodten cliff on foot – sorted by how you get there.

B

  • Spa rule The seaside-resort exception: in season (15 March to 31 October and around Christmas) Travemünde’s shops may open on Sundays too – six hours between 11 am and 7 pm.
  • Accessible Free beach wheelchairs at the Seipel beach-chair rental, accessible beach chairs at Kanthak, accessible toilets with locations and mobility-aid hire in town.
  • Famous faces Thomas Mann called the resort his childhood paradise, Edvard Munch painted here, Willy Brandt fled via the harbour in 1933 – and Travemünde is the birthplace of “Traumschiff” shipowner Peter Deilmann.
  • Boat rental Motorboats up to 15 hp need no licence on the Trave and Baltic; plus harbour tours and party charters on the MS Hanse and self-service SUP hire at the beach.
  • Brodten cliff Over four kilometres of coastal cliff up to 20 metres high between Travemünde and Niendorf – with a path along the edge and one of Europe’s largest sand-martin colonies.

C

  • Camping Strandcamping Priwall (run by the Katt family since 1958), motorhome pitches on the Priwall and at the fishing harbour, and the Ivendorf campsite on the edge of town.

F

  • Fish rolls Freshest at the fishing harbour when the cutters come in each morning; the well-known addresses from Königs Fischbrötchen to Gosch are in the food guide with location and verification date.
  • Fish rolls Freshest at the fishing harbour where the cutters land: Königs Fischbrötchen, Fischkombüse, Kajüte, Fisch Stengel; on the Vorderreihe Gosch and Tiefblau. All stands on a map, plus varieties and World Fish Roll Day.
  • Fun facts Curiosities, records and surprising numbers around the Baltic resort – from Germany’s oldest lighthouse to the once-divided peninsula.

G

  • History First mentioned in 1187, Lübeck’s outpost at the Trave estuary, seaside resort since 1802, incorporated in 1913, divided Priwall until 1990 – as an interactive timeline to today.
  • Golf The Lübeck-Travemünder Golf-Klub of 1921 sits with 27 holes above the Brodten cliff; plus two large complexes in Warnsdorf and Timmendorfer Strand.

H

  • Harbours Five harbours, each with its own page: Skandinavienkai (ferries), Ostpreußenkai (cruise), fishing harbour, Passat harbour with marina and Marina Baltica.
  • Dog beach The designated sections at east Priwall and Brodten are open all year; on the rest of the beach dogs are banned from 1 April to 31 October.

K

  • Kurstrand The main beach in front of the promenade: 2.1 kilometres of fine sand, officially rated “excellent” bathing water, with beach chairs and the DLRG main lifeguard station.

L

  • Lighthouse The Old Lighthouse of 1539 is Germany’s oldest surviving lighthouse – today a museum with a view; since 1974 the active light sits on the Maritim high-rise.

M

  • Maritim high-rise The 119-metre landmark of 1974 carries Europe’s highest lighthouse and has been a protected monument since 2019 as a “beacon of the free West” – a Cold-War sign at the zonal border.
  • With children Playgrounds, climbing forest, sand sculptures and the best rainy-day addresses – sorted by weather, with map and verification date.
  • Museums Four maritime addresses: the museum in the Old Lighthouse, the Seebadmuseum on local history, the museum ship Passat and the sand sculpture exhibition.

O

  • Opening hours Largely free on weekdays, six hours between 11 am and 7 pm on Sundays in season thanks to the spa rule – when and where you can shop.
  • Places The full directory: cafés, restaurants, ice-cream parlours, playgrounds and sights with photos, real aggregated Google ratings and map links.

P

  • Passat The four-masted barque of 1911 is permanently moored on the Priwall bank as a museum ship – Travemünde’s landmark, open in season, with its own marina below.
  • Planner The coast planner builds a day plan from real places, weather and events in 30 seconds – rule-based and transparent, without invented stops.
  • Priwall The roughly three-kilometre peninsula opposite the town centre: natural beach, Passat, marine station and nature reserve – a few minutes away by ferry.

R

  • Cycling Travemünde lies on the Baltic Coast Cycle Route (EuroVelo 10); rewarding rides run along the cliff top, across the Priwall and along the Trave to Lübeck.

S

  • Sea rescue DGzRS volunteers have watched over the Bay of Lübeck since 1865; their boat “Erich Koschubs” (10.1 metres) lies ready in the harbour. Emergency at sea: 124 124.
  • See and do Old Lighthouse, Passat, Vorderreihe, Priwall and cliff – the fixed points with context, plus all sights from the directory.
  • Shopping Small, owner-run shops on the Vorderreihe: bookshop, maritime fashion, jewellery and delicatessen – deliberately no malls, those are in Lübeck.
  • Walks The promenade circuit links the town and Priwall sides via the two ferries into a loop of around two hours – flat, easy walking, from the Nordermole.
  • Beach chairs Four operators on the Kurstrand and Priwall, many chairs bookable online – including sleeping beach chairs and accessible chairs, with contact and hours.

T

  • Travemünde Week One of the world’s big sailing events, every July since 1889: regattas on the Bay of Lübeck, a shore programme, free entry to the festival area.

U

  • About Travemünde Population, area, beach length and the landmarks in verified figures – the resort’s fact sheet, every detail with a source.
  • Accommodation Which location suits which stay – town centre, promenade, Priwall or surroundings: a non-commercial guide, official host directory first.

V

  • Events All dates with date, location, price and source – as a filterable list or as dedicated month pages from June to December.

W

  • Water level The Travemünde gauge live from Pegelonline – and why the Baltic is practically tide-free, with wind driving high and low water.
  • Water temperature The current Baltic reading, updated several times a day – with day preview, official bathing-water status and the yearly pattern as a monthly table.
  • Wellness Day spa without an overnight stay: sauna with a Baltic view at the aja SEEBAD and the roughly 600 m² spa at the ATLANTIC Grand Hotel.
  • Weekly market Year-round on Mondays and Thursdays from 7:30 am to 1 pm at the Fährplatz – fresh regional produce right by the ferry landing, in any weather.
