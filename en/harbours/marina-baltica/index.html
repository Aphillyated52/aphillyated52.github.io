Where the Trave widens and calms down lies Marina Baltica – less shop window, more home port. For crews the position is practical: sheltered from the bay’s swell, with the big ships as neighbours on the way out to sea.

By land it is a short hop into town; on the water, the quiet cruising ground of the Pötenitzer Wiek begins just beyond the marina.

For getting there: the marina sits at Travemünder Landstraße 300, in the town’s south-west between the fishing harbour and Skandinavienkai – by water roughly one nautical mile upriver of the estuary, sheltered in a bay of the Trave. The operator runs it as a full-service harbour with boatyards next door; ask there directly for binding details on berths, crane and winter storage.

Local tip: From the water, the evening run is the treat: down the Trave with the last light – the lit-up Vorderreihe appears right on cue.

Good to know

  • Ask the operator directly about berths, crane and services.
  • By train: the “Lübeck-Skandinavienkai” stop is closest.
  • On the water: cross the big-ship fairway, do not linger in it.

Frequently asked questions

Where is Marina Baltica?

Upriver on the lower Trave, where the river widens and calms – sheltered from the bay’s swell, with a short hop into town and towards the Pötenitzer Wiek.

How do I reach Marina Baltica without a boat?

By train the “Lübeck-Skandinavienkai” stop is closest, a few minutes away. Live departures are on the getting-here page.

Are there berths and a crane?

Berths, crane and services are handled by the operator – binding information only from them. We deliberately do not quote invented berth or fee figures.
