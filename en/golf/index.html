Golf in Travemünde and the Bay of Lübeck

Yes, you can play golf in Travemünde – at one of the most established courses on the German Baltic coast. The Lübeck-Travemünde Golf Club has sat high above the Brodten cliffs since 1921, with 27 holes and wide views over the Bay of Lübeck. For a golf holiday on the Baltic, the distances are short here: beach, promenade and golf course are all in the same town.

Around Travemünde, the Bay of Lübeck adds two more large facilities – the Maritim Golfpark Ostsee near Warnsdorf with 33 holes and the Seeschlösschen course in Timmendorfer Strand with 36 holes. Together that is almost a hundred fairways within about fifteen minutes, from a championship course to a 6-hole short course for beginners. Guests with a DGV card are welcome at all three; book the green fee and tee time directly with each club.

What golfers want to know

Green fee & tee time

Book directly with the club. The Lübeck-Travemünde club plays without fixed tee times and its three loops are freely combinable; green fees depend on summer or winter greens.

Guests & handicap

All three courses welcome guests with a DGV card. At the LTGK, handicap up to 36 plays freely, 37–54 only accompanied – allow a little lead time for your first round.

Practice & first swings

Driving ranges at the LTGK (with sea views) and at Seeschlösschen (heated bays). The 6-hole short course at Maritim Golfpark is a good place to start.

Getting there

Via the A1 to Lübeck, then the B75; the LTGK is in the Brodten district above the cliffs. By train to Travemünde, the last stretch by car or taxi.

Frequently asked questions about golf in Travemünde

Can you play golf in Travemünde?

Yes. The Lübeck-Travemünde Golf Club, founded in 1921, is right in Travemünde above the Brodten cliffs and offers 27 holes with views over the Baltic. Two more large facilities sit nearby.

How many golf courses are there in the Bay of Lübeck around Travemünde?

Three larger facilities within reach: the Lübeck-Travemünde Golf Club (27 holes), the Maritim Golfpark Ostsee in Warnsdorf (33 holes) and the Seeschlösschen course in Timmendorfer Strand (36 holes).

Which golf course has sea views?

The Lübeck-Travemünde Golf Club sits high above the cliff coast; many fairways and the driving range look out over the Baltic. The Maritim Golfpark, by contrast, lies inland by the Hemmelsdorfer See.

Can guests play without membership, and what handicap is required?

Guests with a DGV card are welcome. At the Lübeck-Travemünde club, handicap up to 36 plays freely, 37–54 accompanied by a member. Book the green fee and tee time directly with the club.

Is there a golf course for beginners?

The Maritim Golfpark Ostsee has a 6-hole short course that is well suited to starting out. Driving ranges for practice are available at the Lübeck-Travemünde club and at Seeschlösschen (with heated bays).

What is the golf season like in the Bay of Lübeck?

Play is year-round. The Lübeck-Travemünde club tiers its green fees by summer and winter greens, with the competition season running April to October. Current times and prices are on the club websites.
