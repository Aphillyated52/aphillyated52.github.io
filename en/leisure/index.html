    aja SEEBAD (Day Spa)

    Alter Leuchtturm – Besteigung

    142 steps, eight museum floors and a panoramic view over bay and estuary at the top. The tower is considered Germany’s oldest lighthouse – first documented in 1226.

    July/August daily 11–16, otherwise 13–16 · adults €4, children €2

    Kletterwald Travemünde

    Seven courses with 60 platforms in the woods by Parkallee – from the crab course for the youngest (from around age 3) to the high routes, with a café for those waiting. The social programme “Keine Knete” lets children from low-income families climb for free.

    April–October daily 10–18 (March 11–17) · children from €13, adults from €20, season pass €125 · Parkallee 3

Good to know – what is no longer there

  • There is no cinema in Travemünde – movie night means a trip to Lübeck.
  • Classic mini golf disappeared from town in 2013; today you play adventure golf on the Priwall and blacklight mini golf indoors. The city is planning a new course in the Brügmanngarten “Sportgarten” (planning resolution January 2026).
  • The former casino closed in 2012 – the building now houses the ATLANTIC Grand Hotel.
  • The angling boat MS Karoline now sails from Fehmarn, no longer from Niendorf.

Sources: HL-live (Sportgarten, January 2026), operator information – as of 10 June 2026.

Seasonal in the winter half-year

Once the beach chairs are in storage: the “SCHIFF AHOI” Christmas market (late November to early January), the winter world in the Brügmanngarten, beach rides from the riding stable (15 September to 1 May) and the ice-bathing season, opened by the traditional first swim. There is no ice rink in Travemünde – but there is the Eiswelt in Scharbeutz.

Common questions about leisure in Travemünde

What can you do in Travemünde when it rains?

In rain, the PriWilly Cosmos indoor play hall on the Priwall, the Seebadmuseum and the day spa on the promenade work well; the four-masted barque Passat can be visited under cover too. For longer rainy days, Sea Life Timmendorfer Strand and the OstseeTherme Scharbeutz are each about 20 minutes away.

Is there a cinema in Travemünde?

No. Travemünde has no cinema – the nearest movie night is in Lübeck.

Is there mini golf in Travemünde?

Classic mini golf has been gone since 2013. Today you play adventure golf on the Priwall and blacklight mini golf indoors. The city is planning a new course in the Brügmanngarten.

Is there a casino in Travemünde?

No. The former casino closed in 2012; the building now houses the ATLANTIC Grand Hotel.
