What to do when it rains?

The most reliable indoor address is PriWilly Cosmos on the Priwall (under this name since 1 April 2026, formerly BayCenter) – indoor adventure playground, blacklight mini golf and a laser maze under one roof. The Ostseestation by the Trave and the Seebadmuseum are good for an hour in the dry; the four-masted barque Passat can be visited in showers too.

Frequently asked questions

What to do in Travemünde with children when it rains?

The PriWilly Cosmos indoor play hall on the Priwall, the Ostseestation by the Trave, the Seebadmuseum – and the Passat can be visited in rain as well. The ferry ride across the Trave is a small adventure in itself.

Which playground is right on the beach?

The beach playground on the Kurstrand sits directly on the sand, within sight of the promenade. On the Priwall, the playground at the Priwallpromenade is similarly close to the water.

Is the climbing forest suitable for small children?

The climbing forest has courses at different heights; suitability depends on age and height – binding details are on the operator’s linked website.

Does the beach cost anything for families?

Travemünde charges a resort fee in season; current rates are listed on the official tourism site travemuende-tourismus.de.

Which day trips nearby are worth it with children?

Sea Life Timmendorfer Strand and the Vogelpark Niendorf are about 15 minutes away and good for a rainy day. Karls Erlebnis-Dorf in Warnsdorf (approx. 20 min) has free entry; Hansa-Park in Sierksdorf (approx. 25 min) is the region’s largest theme park. Opening hours and prices on the operators’ sites.

From what age is Travemünde suitable for children?

The gently sloping Kurstrand suits every age – toddlers paddle in the shallow water. From school age come the climbing forest (mind the minimum height), the laser maze at PriWilly Cosmos and boat trips on the Trave.

How do you get to Travemünde with children?

From Lübeck the train runs directly to Lübeck-Travemünde Strand; by car via the A1 and B75, parking near the beach. Details and live departures are on the getting-here page.
