Which fish roll? The varieties

Matjes

Young, mildly salted herring – traditionally with just raw onions. The tender classic.

Bismarck herring

Herring pickled in vinegar, oil and onions – named after Chancellor Bismarck, with gherkin and onion.

Fried herring

Fried and then soured – heartier and firmer than matjes.

Fried fish

Battered, deep-fried fillet (often pollock or cod) – warm, crisp, with remoulade.

Brown shrimp

North Sea brown shrimp, plain or with egg – the fine, slightly pricier option.

Salmon and smoked fish

Cold-smoked salmon or mackerel – mild and without acidity, good for beginners.

World Fish Roll Day

Since 2011 Schleswig-Holstein has celebrated World Fish Roll Day on the first Saturday in May – an initiative of Ostsee-Holstein-Tourismus that celebrates fishing, maritime culture and the stand next door. The coast holds special events; in 2026 the day falls on 2 May. A good reason to work your way through Travemünde’s stands.

A short history of the fish roll

The fish roll comes from the north-German port cities of the 19th century: a quick, cheap meal for fishermen, sailors and dockworkers, eaten standing up. Because fresh fish spoils quickly, it was preserved – salted, pickled, smoked. That gave rise to matjes, Bismarck herring and rollmops, still the basis of the topped roll today. A workers’ snack became a cultural staple of the whole coast.

Frequently asked questions about fish rolls in Travemünde

Where are the best fish rolls in Travemünde?

Freshest at the fishing harbour on the Baggersand (Königs Fischbrötchen, Fischkombüse, Kajüte, Fisch Stengel), plus Gosch and Tiefblau on the Vorderreihe. “Best” is a matter of taste – we don’t invent a ranking; real ratings are in the places directory.

Which fish-roll varieties are there?

The classics are matjes (mild, with onions), Bismarck herring (pickled), fried herring, battered fried fish (warm), brown shrimp and smoked salmon or mackerel. For beginners the mild smoked-fish versions are easiest.

When is World Fish Roll Day?

World Fish Roll Day has taken place on the first Saturday in May since 2011; in 2026 that is 2 May. The Baltic coast holds special events around fresh fish.

Where can I get fresh fish straight from the cutter?

At the fishing harbour on the Baggersand: the cutters usually return around 8 a.m., and the catch is sold straight from the boat or at stalls on land. There are no fixed sales times.
