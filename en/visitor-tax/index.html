Spa levy for overnight guests

Per person and overnight stay, adults from 18. The spa levy is usually collected via the host (hotel, holiday flat).

Periodper person and day
1 January – 14 May2,00 €
15 May – 14 September (high season)3,50 €
15 September – 31 December2,00 €
Children’s card (up to and incl. 17)1,20 €

Beach fee for day visitors

Anyone visiting the beach without staying overnight pays a day fee in the high season (15 May to 14 September). Day beach cards are available at machines or from the beach-chair rentals at the beach entrances.

Periodper person and day
15 May – 14 September, from 163,50 €
same period, from 3 p.m.2,00 €

Children and reductions

Children and young people aged 4 up to and including 17 pay nothing and receive the ostseecard free on request. For children up to 17 there is also a reduced children’s card at 1,20 € per day, if one is to be issued. Children under 4 are free anyway.

What does the ostseecard give you?

The ostseecard is proof of the paid spa levy and unlocks access to the managed beach sections and further spa services. The exact services are listed by the tourism agency; the legally binding rules – e.g. on residents, payment methods and consequences of non-payment – are set by the Hansestadt Lübeck by-law.

Frequently asked questions about the visitor’s tax

What does the visitor’s tax cost in Travemünde?

Overnight guests pay 3,50 € per person per day in the high season (15 May to 14 September) and 2,00 € in the low season. Day visitors pay a 3,50 € beach fee in the high season, 2,00 € from 3 p.m.

From what age do you pay the visitor’s tax?

Children and young people aged 4 up to and including 17 are free from the spa levy. The day-visitor beach fee applies from 16. Children under 4 are always free.

Are children exempt from the visitor’s tax?

Yes. Children and young people up to and including 17 pay no spa levy and receive the ostseecard free on request. Optionally there is a reduced children’s card at 1,20 €.

Do Lübeck residents have to pay the visitor’s tax?

The spa levy concerns guests. Whether and for whom there are exemptions for residents is set by the Hansestadt Lübeck spa-levy by-law – that is where the binding answer is.

How and where do you pay the visitor’s tax?

Overnight guests usually pay the spa levy via their host. Day visitors buy a day beach card at a machine or from the beach-chair rentals at the entrances. Check details and any online payment with the spa administration or tourism agency.

What do you get for the visitor’s tax?

The ostseecard unlocks access to the managed beach sections and further spa services. It is also proof that the contribution has been paid.
