Promenade and paths

The Travepromenade by the harbour is flat and wide and can be used well with a wheelchair or walker; from here the tourist information, ferry landing and many shops are reachable. For individual beach accesses and ramps: design and condition can vary by section and season – follow the signage on site and, if in doubt, ask the tourist information.

Low-barrier arrival and parking

Travemünde is reachable by train (Travemünde Strand station is right on the promenade), by bus and by car. Accessible parking spaces and step-free routes change seasonally – current locations, stops and parking are best planned via the arrival page with live departures and via the tourist information.

The official accessibility information also offers content in sign language and in easy-to-read German. The tourist information (phone 0451 8899700, Travepromenade) helps with further questions and current details.

Frequently asked questions about accessible Travemünde

Is there a beach wheelchair to borrow in Travemünde?

Yes, free of charge: the Seipel beach-chair rental issues beach swim wheelchairs that reach the shallow water. Best to ask about availability in advance.

Where are accessible toilets in Travemünde?

Among others at the Musikmuschel (Brügmanngarten), the DLRG main station (Kaiserallee 6), the Stadtbäckerei and Fisch Paul pavilions (Travepromenade) and on the Priwall (Waldweg 94/25a, Dünenweg 15). The Euro toilet key can be borrowed against a €30 deposit at the tourist information.

Are there accessible beach chairs in Travemünde?

Yes, accessible beach chairs are available at the Kanthak beach-chair rental; they can be reserved through its booking.

Can you hire wheelchairs or electric scooters in Travemünde?

Yes. Wheelchairs are hired by Sanitätshaus Ostsee Medizintechnik, electric scooters by Grall’s Mobilitätshaus; MeinHilfsmittel (Sopamo GmbH) also arranges mobility aids.
